Faisal Mosque

★ 4.9 (2,184 reviews) 📍 Islamabad, Pakistan Top Pick
About
Highlights
Travel Tips

Faisal Mosque, the national mosque of Pakistan, is one of the largest mosques in the world. Located at the foot of the Margalla Hills in Islamabad, this architectural marvel was designed by Turkish architect Vedat Dalokay and completed in 1986.

With its unique contemporary design inspired by a Bedouin tent, the mosque can accommodate up to 100,000 worshippers across its main prayer hall, courtyard, and porticoes. The four towering minarets and stunning marble façade make it a must-visit landmark for both spiritual seekers and architecture enthusiasts.

🧣 Dress modestly — both men and women should cover shoulders and knees. Women are required to cover their heads inside the prayer hall.

🕐 Best time to visit: Early morning or just before sunset to avoid crowds and enjoy the best lighting for photographs.

🚗 Parking: Free parking available on-site. Allow 1.5–2 hours for a complete visit including prayer hall and surrounding gardens.
